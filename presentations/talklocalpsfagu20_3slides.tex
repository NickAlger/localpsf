\vspace{2em}

\begin{itemize}
	\setlength\itemsep{1em}
	\item \textbf{Goal:} Infer basal sliding friction field for ice sheet using surface velocity observations, and quantify uncertainty
	\item \textbf{Problem:} Existing methods are based on low-rank Hessian approximation, but Hessian is high rank!
\end{itemize}
\end{center}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
  \frametitle{Key result}

Product-convolution approximation combined with Hierarchical matrix representations promise a more efficient
Hessian approximation.

\vspace{1em}

\begin{center}
	\includegraphics[width=0.75\columnwidth]{extraplots/ice_gmres_convergence.pdf}
\end{center}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
	\frametitle{Broader impact}

\begin{itemize}
	\setlength\itemsep{1em}
	\item Antarctic ice sheet flow is expected to be the dominant contributor to 21st century sea level rise.
	\item A leading source of uncertainty is the basal sliding friction.
	\item Our Hessian approximation reduces the computational cost of estimating the basal sliding friction and quantifying its uncertainty.
\end{itemize}
